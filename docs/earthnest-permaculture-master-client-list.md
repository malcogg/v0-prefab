# EarthNest full-stack permaculture & eco client master list

**Build configurator:** Checkbox labels, module grouping, and option IDs for the Customize → **EarthNest & site** tab live in [`lib/build/eco-modules.ts`](../lib/build/eco-modules.ts). When you add or change offerings here, update that file in the same pass so the site stays in sync.

This document is the **working master list** of products, systems, materials, and design intents clients may pursue when they want the **most complete** Prefabricated.co / **EarthNest Living Systems** expression: a home that stacks **shelter, energy, water, food, soil health, outdoor living, and light monitoring** in a code-aware way.

**How to use this doc**

- Treat each block as a **module**. Clients rarely buy “everything” at once; use **phasing** (see below).
- Items mix **(A) things you can buy / specify**, **(B) design/engineering packages**, and **(C) site work**—note the column in your CRM or scope doc.
- **Add rows** under any module; keep one line per discrete choice so the build configurator can import this later.

**Disclaimers (non-negotiable in sales copy)**

- EarthNest is a **concept and systems map**, not a guarantee that every item is legal, insurable, or wise on a given parcel. **Zoning, HOA, flood, septic vs sewer, setbacks, and Florida Building Code** decide what is allowed.
- “Permaculture” here means **regenerative land use + efficient building systems**, not a certification. Use **local designers** (civil, landscape, MEP) where required.
- **Humidity markets (Florida):** prioritize **ventilation, dehumidification, drainage, and mold prevention**. “Natural” finishes do not replace a good moisture strategy.

## Phasing: typical client path

| Phase | Goal | Client takeaway |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **0 — Baseline** | Legal, durable small home / ADU shell | Code-compliant structure; efficient envelope baseline from factory/Builder |
| **1 — Eco-ready shell** | Low operating cost, healthy interior | Solar-ready electrical, efficient HVAC, low-VOC finishes, storm-smart details |
| **2 — Water & landscape** | Irrigation, stormwater, soil | Roofwater for irrigation where allowed, Florida-friendly plant palette, compost |
| **3 — Full stack** | Food + monitoring + resilience | Edible layers, advanced water *where permitted*, backup power, sensors |

**Full permaculture / full EarthNest (this list)** ≈ Phases **1–3** implemented as far as the **site** allows—not a single shopping cart.

## 1. Envelope & structure

High performance and durability; many items are **assembly-dependent** (factory vs site-built).

| Item | Type | Notes |
|------|------|--------|
| Engineered wood / CLT structural elements (where applicable) | Specify / Buy | Verify supplier docs & insurance for Florida loads |
| FSC or documented responsible lumber siding & trim | Buy | Termite & finish strategy in humid climates |
| Fiber-cement or metal cladding (cool roof / reflective options) | Buy | Pair with rain screen & detailing per engineer |
| High-performance windows (low U-value, appropriate SHGC) | Buy | Impact rating where required; shading strategy |
| Insulation: dense-pack cellulose, wood-fiber, mineral wool (per assembly) | Specify | **Moisture-safe** assembly first; avoid “more R” without a plan |
| Continuous air barrier + taped/liquid-flashed penetrations | Site work / Specify | Critical in humid cooling climates |
| Airtight electrical/plumbing penetrations detail package | Specify | Cheap insurance against moisture issues |
| Roof: cool roof / light color / reflective membrane (as appropriate) | Buy / Specify | Coordinate with solar attachment & wind rating |
| Hurricane strapping / connector schedule (as required) | Buy / Specify | Non-optional in much of Florida |
| Exterior shading: overhangs, fixed louvers, awnings | Buy / Specify | Often better ROI than extra glass area |
| Limewash / mineral exterior finishes (where substrate allows) | Buy / Study first | Compatibility with WRB and cladding system |

**Design intents (often not a SKu):** thermal mass vs insulation balance; orientation; compact form factor—document in **site evaluation**.
