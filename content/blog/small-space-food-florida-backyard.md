## Why this matters for Florida micro-lots

Backyard ADUs, cottage rentals, and tiny-home pads often share the same constraint: **usable outdoor space is measured in strips and corners, not acres**. Food production is still realistic when you treat the yard as a **stacked system**—canopy, shrub, herb layer, and vertical climbers—rather than a miniature farm.

We are not replacing Ag Extension or your county’s fertilizer guidance. Use this as a planning lens alongside [UF/IFAS Gardening Solutions](https://gardeningsolutions.ifas.ufl.edu/) and local ordinances.

## Sun, heat, and microclimates

- Track **morning vs afternoon sun** along fences and the ADU wall; reflected heat from paving and metal roofing changes leaf burn risk.
- **Afternoon shade** in summer often beats “more sun” for leafy greens; plan seasonal rotations.
- **Airflow** matters as much as sun in humid builds; avoid cramming plants into dead-air pockets against siding (pest and mold pressure).

## Soil and containers

- **Raised beds and modular planters** keep roots above compaction and make it easier to amend soil deliberately—especially on former turf or fill lots.
- **Depth rule of thumb**: shallow roots (greens) need less depth than tomatoes, peppers, or sweet potatoes. Match container depth to the crop list you actually eat.
- Import soil/compost from reputable local blends when you can; bagged mixes vary widely—label literacy beats brand loyalty.

## Water: mulch, rhythm, backups

Florida’s rainfall is flashy; **evaporation is steady**. Mulch is your first irrigation “device.”

- Water **deeply and less often** once plants are established, adapted to pot vs in-ground context.
- **Timers and simple drip** help during travel and heat waves; pair written schedules with a visual check so you notice clogging emitters or blown-off lines.
- **Sub-irrigation tricks** (ollas, capillary mats) can buffer missed waterings but do not replace planning for dry weeks.

## Go vertical without annoying the neighbor

- **Tomato cages, trellises, and panels** buy square footage on fences and bed corners—keep height and shed rules in mind if you share a property line.
- Train vining crops **away from gutters and soffits**; keep harvest paths clear for maintenance access around the ADU.

## Rules, leases, and realism

- **HOAs** may restrict visible structures, ground coverage, or “agricultural” appearances; read covenants before investing in large beds.
- **Renters**: confirm what can be installed, removed, and restored at move-out; prioritize portable containers if the lease is short.
- **Nutrient and pesticide runoff** can affect stormwater—keep buffers, follow label law, and avoid spraying uphill of shared drains.
